SKYN KING Digital Campaign

Overview

SKYN, the #1 condom seller on Amazon, aimed to disrupt the condom industry by repositioning its SKYN KING line as a premium alternative designed specifically for Black men. The vision extended beyond product differentiation. The brand sought to connect emotionally with an audience navigating confidence, vulnerability, and identity within the broader cultural landscape.

I contributed to the visual and digital execution of this initiative across Amazon storefront optimization and paid advertising, balancing emotional storytelling, product differentiation, and strict platform compliance requirements.

Challenge

Cultural Positioning
The campaign targeted Black men experiencing a duality of power and vulnerability. The creative needed to feel authentic, respectful, and aspirational without relying on stereotypes or surface-level representation.

Category Sensitivity
Condom advertising has strict restrictions across Amazon and digital ad platforms. Visual presentation must avoid explicit imagery while still communicating product benefits such as size, comfort, and intimacy.

Platform Constraints
Amazon storefront layouts and ad placements impose rigid formatting and content guidelines. Every design decision had to comply while still standing out in a highly competitive category.

Competitive Disruption
Magnum dominated the “larger-size” condom segment. SKYN KING needed to clearly communicate superior product attributes:

  • Larger and longer fit
  • Thinner material
  • Latex-free SKYNFEEL
  • Superior skin-on-skin comfort

Research & Strategic Framing

I aligned with stakeholders on audience insights and brand positioning. Identifying a foundation would shape tone, visual hierarchy, and messaging across storefront and advertising assets.

Target Audience
Black men navigating a sense of strength and vulnerability, seeking premium products that align with identity and aspiration. The brand opportunity was to position SKYN KING as:

  • Fit for a King
  • Freedom to Feel
  • Masters of Pleasure
  • A product for connection and intimacy, not just performance

Design Execution

Amazon Storefront Optimization
I worked on visual refinement of product presentation within Amazon’s compliance constraints. This included:

  • Clear hierarchy of product differentiators
  • Emotional storytelling through copy and imagery
  • Visual emphasis on size, comfort, and premium positioning
  • Non-explicit presentation aligned with Amazon guidelines

Social Media Campaign Assets
Created culturally relevant, emotionally grounded social content that positioned SKYN KING as both premium and relatable. This included:

  • Platform-specific creative tailored for Instagram and digital placements
  • Visual storytelling that reinforced vulnerability, confidence, and connection
  • Messaging that spoke directly to the Black male demographic without stereotyping
  • Consistent brand tone across static posts, motion assets, and campaign rollouts

Paid Advertising Creative
Developed ad creative that balanced:

  • Emotional resonance with the target audience
  • Clear communication of functional superiority
  • Compliance with platform restrictions
  • Scroll-stopping visual clarity without explicit content

Impact and Results

  • Successfully launched live Amazon storefront and paid media assets that directly influenced high-intent purchase behavior in a competitive category.
  • Contributed to a 18% increase in Amazon product page conversion rate by restructuring visual hierarchy and clarifying premium differentiators within strict compliance guidelines.
  • Repositioned SKYN KING from a functional product alternative to a culturally relevant, premium brand platform centered on intimacy, confidence, and emotional intelligence.
  • Drove a 21% increase in paid social engagement by aligning campaign visuals with culturally resonant messaging tailored to the Black male demographic.

Conclusion

This work reinforced that design in sensitive, competitive categories requires more than aesthetic execution. It requires cultural awareness, compliance literacy, strategic messaging, and performance thinking.

Balancing emotional positioning, strict ad guidelines, and competitive disruption strategy pushed me to think beyond interface design and operate at the intersection of brand, culture, and commerce.
